Rehabilitation Therapy FAQ

Answers to your most common questions about rehabilitation therapy and recovery at Optalis.

General Questions

About Rehabilitation

What types of therapy are offered at Optalis?
Optalis offers comprehensive rehabilitation services including physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech-language therapy. Our therapists work together to create a personalized treatment plan focused on helping you return home safely.
How long does rehabilitation typically last?
The length of your rehabilitation stay depends on your individual needs and goals. Most patients stay between 10-21 days, but some may require shorter or longer stays. Your care team will work with you and your family to determine the best plan.
What's the difference between inpatient and outpatient rehab?
Inpatient rehabilitation means you stay at our facility 24/7 while receiving therapy and nursing care. Outpatient therapy allows you to live at home and come to therapy sessions during the day. Your doctor will recommend which option is best based on your condition and support system at home.
How many hours of therapy will I receive each day?
Most patients receive between 1-3 hours of therapy per day, depending on their condition, stamina, and treatment plan. Therapy may include physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy sessions spread throughout the day.
Can my family visit during my rehabilitation stay?
Yes! We encourage family involvement in the rehabilitation process. Visitors are welcome during visiting hours, and family members can often participate in therapy sessions to learn how to help with your ongoing recovery at home.
Insurance & Cost

Coverage Questions

Does Medicare cover rehabilitation?
Yes, Medicare typically covers skilled nursing and rehabilitation services after a qualifying hospital stay of at least 3 consecutive days. Medicare covers 100% of the first 20 days, then there is a daily co-pay for days 21-100. Our admissions team can help you understand your specific coverage.
What if I have a Medicare Advantage plan?
Medicare Advantage plans vary in their coverage. Many plans cover skilled nursing rehabilitation, but benefits may differ from traditional Medicare. Contact your plan directly or speak with our admissions team to verify your benefits.
Is the 3-day hospital stay rule always required?
For traditional Medicare, yes - you need a qualifying 3-day inpatient hospital stay. However, some Medicare Advantage plans have waived this requirement. Private insurance and other payers may have different requirements.
What happens if I need to stay longer than Medicare covers?
If you need additional care beyond what Medicare covers, our social services team will work with you and your family to explore options, including Medicaid, private pay, or other resources. We'll never discharge you without a safe plan in place.
Daily Life

What to Expect

What should I bring to rehabilitation?
Bring comfortable, loose-fitting clothes and supportive shoes for therapy sessions. Also bring personal toiletries, any medical equipment you use (glasses, hearing aids, dentures), a list of medications, and a few personal items from home. Leave valuables at home.
What are the meals like?
We offer restaurant-style dining with a variety of nutritious meal options. Our dietary team can accommodate special diets, allergies, and preferences. Many patients are pleasantly surprised by the quality and variety of our food.
Can I continue taking my regular medications?
Yes, our nursing staff will manage all your medications during your stay. Bring a complete list of your current medications (including over-the-counter drugs and supplements) so we can coordinate your care with your physicians.
What happens after I'm discharged?
Before discharge, our team will create a comprehensive plan for your continued recovery at home. This may include home health services, outpatient therapy, equipment needs, and follow-up appointments. We'll make sure you and your family feel confident about the transition.

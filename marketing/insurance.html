Understanding Your Coverage

Managed Care Insurance Benefits

Learn how managed care insurance benefits can help cover the cost of your rehabilitation, skilled nursing, or assisted living care.

The Basics

What Is a Managed Care Insurance Benefit?

A managed care insurance benefit is a plan that contracts with healthcare providers to provide care at a reduced cost. These plans help control costs while ensuring a high quality of care.

If you have a managed care insurance plan, you might want to know what coverage it provides for independent living, assisted living, skilled nursing, or a rehabilitation stay.

Know Your Coverage

Understanding Your Benefits

The type of plans and the benefits vary widely, so it's important to learn the ins and outs of your insurance benefit before needing it.

We highly recommend contacting your insurance provider directly for details about your specific coverage. Our admissions team can also help you navigate your benefits.

By providing your insurance policy number and date of birth, our director of admissions can help determine what benefits are available to you at our communities.

Contact Admissions
Plan Types

Types of Managed Care Insurance Plans

Commercial/Employer Plans

Plans through your employer, often called group-sponsored health plans. Common examples include HMOs (Health Maintenance Organizations) and PPOs (Preferred Provider Organizations).

Medicare Advantage

Medicare-approved private companies typically offer coverage for services that your original Medicare plan doesn't cover, often including additional benefits.

Medicaid Managed Care

Ohio's Medicaid Managed Care includes therapy services, respite care, skilled nursing services, and more for eligible individuals.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I determine my managed care insurance plan benefits?

You can contact your insurance provider directly, or reach out to the director of admissions at the Optalis community of your choice. They'll use your policy number and date of birth to determine what benefits are available to you. You must meet an identified skilled level of care for your insurance benefit to provide coverage.

Does Optalis accept my managed care insurance?

Yes, Optalis communities accept most managed care insurance benefits. However, it does vary by plan and insurance company, so it's important to contact your insurance company directly or reach out to us to determine your coverage.

What happens during my stay?

Once we submit prior authorization and your managed care insurance company approves it, the Optalis community team will complete weekly continued stay reviews. When the managed care insurance company determines your stay is no longer necessary, they will let us know, and we will notify you and coordinate a discharge plan.

What if my insurance says my stay is no longer necessary?

If your managed care insurance company feels that your skilled nursing and rehabilitation stay is no longer necessary, you have the right to appeal this decision. Your Optalis community team can help you with this process and also help you find an appropriate next level of care, such as outpatient rehabilitation or our assisted living rehab and recovery program.

Still Have Questions?

Contact us today. One of our experts will help you with questions specific to your individual needs and financial situation.

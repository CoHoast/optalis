Inpatient Rehabilitation Services

Inpatient rehabilitation is typically for people who go from the hospital to a rehabilitation center, where they'll work with a therapy team for parts of each day and then rest and enjoy the center's activities for the remainder of the day.

What to Expect When You Stay With Us

  • 1
    Initial Assessment

    An initial assessment is completed upon admission. Assessments last 30-60 minutes while therapists discuss your level of function before admission and evaluate your current status.

  • 2
    Personalized Plan of Care

    A plan of care will be established centered on your personal goals and functional abilities.

  • 3
    Daily Therapy Sessions

    Therapy is offered up to seven days a week, based on your individual needs and physical condition. Therapy sessions are generally 30-90 minutes in length per discipline.

  • 4
    Interdisciplinary Team Approach

    Therapists work with an interdisciplinary team, which includes your physician, to monitor progress and make recommendations to maximize your recovery.

  • 5
    Home Preparation

    In-home therapy assessments are available when appropriate to ensure a smooth transition back to your home environment.

Preparation Tips

We recommend wearing loose-fitting clothing that allows movement and proper footwear during therapy sessions.

Therapy sessions: 30-90 minutes

Length of Stay

The average length of stay varies based on individual needs and progress. Our team works diligently to help you achieve your recovery goals as efficiently as possible.

Flexible Care

Outpatient Rehabilitation Services

Outpatient rehabilitation is for people who need some type of physical, occupational, or speech therapy but can live at home while traveling to a rehabilitation center to receive services.

Services Include:

Assistance with balance
Pain management
Eating or swallowing assistance
Self-care challenges with bathing or dressing
Mobility/ambulating

Outpatient vs. Inpatient Rehab

Understanding the difference between outpatient and inpatient rehab can help you make the best decision for your recovery journey.

Inpatient Rehabilitation

  • Stay within the community until ready to return home
  • Therapy on campus with variety of services and amenities
  • Also referred to as post-hospital inpatient rehab
  • Ideal for those needing help recovering after a hospital stay

Outpatient Rehabilitation

  • Come in for therapy sessions, then return home
  • Maintain independence while receiving care
  • Flexible scheduling around your life
  • Physical, occupational, or speech therapy as needed

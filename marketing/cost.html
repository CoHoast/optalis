Understanding Cost & Payment Options

Confused about what insurance covers? We're here to help you navigate the costs associated with assisted living, rehabilitation, and skilled nursing.

Rehabilitation Cost Questions

Most rehabilitation centers charge a daily rate. The average cost of a skilled nursing and rehabilitation center is approximately $280 per day for a semi-private room and $319 for a private room.

Medicare Coverage for Rehabilitation

Most Medicare plans cover up to 100 days of rehab if you meet the guidelines, although the average length of stay is approximately 30 days.

  • Days 1-20: Covered by Medicare at 100%
  • Days 21-100: Daily copay required if you still qualify for care
  • After Day 100: You are responsible for full costs

Does Medicaid Cover Rehabilitation?

If you qualify, Medicaid does help cover rehabilitation needed due to injury, disability, or illness. Medicaid also covers skilled nursing services at Medicaid-certified communities as long as the patient meets certain eligibility requirements.

What's Included in Rehab Costs?

While receiving inpatient rehabilitation services at an Optalis care community, patients have access to:

  • Therapy and nursing services
  • Meals and activities
  • Personal laundry and linen services
  • Activities and outings
  • Transportation

Assisted Living Cost Questions

The cost of assisted living depends on the community you choose and the apartment type. The average monthly cost of an assisted living community is approximately $4,774.

At Optalis, monthly rates vary based on two factors:

  1. The apartment rate
  2. The level of care rate (determined by assessment prior to moving in)

Monthly rates at Optalis assisted living communities typically range from $4,000-$5,000.

Does Medicare Cover Assisted Living?

Medicare does not cover assisted living. Most assisted living communities are private-pay, and most private health insurance plans do not cover the cost of assisted living.

What About Medicaid for Assisted Living?

Some assisted living communities accept the Ohio Medicaid assisted living waiver, which can help with costs. However, the resident must first have completed two or more years of private pay before this option is available.

Long-Term Care Cost Questions

The cost of long-term care varies with each resident. Because of differing needs and levels of care, it's best to contact us for a specific cost range.

Medicare & Long-Term Care

Medicare will not cover extended stays in long-term care. However, if you have recently been hospitalized for three or more nights, Medicare may cover all or part of the cost of skilled nursing for up to 100 days.

What's Included in Long-Term Care?

  • Rooms with in-room amenities
  • Personal laundry and linen service
  • Activities and outings
  • Dining and community spaces
  • Personal billing assistance

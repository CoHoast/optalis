Preparing for Your Stay

What to Expect During Your Rehabilitation Stay

Preparing for an upcoming rehabilitation and therapy stay can feel challenging. Be assured that our team is ready and prepared to welcome you warmly.

Your Therapy Journey

Upon arrival, you will be greeted warmly by your nurse and nursing assistant. Both will be well-informed of your current condition and specific care needs. They will complete an initial health assessment and answer any questions you may have.

Initial Assessment

  • Assessments last 30-60 minutes while therapists discuss your level of function before admission and evaluate your current status
  • A plan of care will be established centered on your personal goals and functional abilities
  • Therapy is offered up to seven days a week, based on your individual needs
  • Therapy sessions are generally 30-90 minutes in length per discipline

What to Pack

Please consider bringing:

  • Comfortable clothing and sweatsuits
  • Tennis shoes or walking shoes
  • Socks and undergarments
  • Pajamas and a robe
  • Glasses, hearing aids, and dentures
  • Adaptive equipment (walkers, canes)
  • Personal care items
  • Cell phone and charger

Please leave at home: Large amounts of money, valuables, and jewelry.

Visiting Hours

Visitors are welcome. We recommend visitation within normal business hours of 7 AM – 7 PM.

Dining

For lunch and dinner, we have a "special of the day" option. You may choose to enjoy your meals in our well-appointed dining room or have your meals delivered to your room. We also have a Bistro Menu that offers many delicious choices.

Length of Stay

Medicare pays for a portion of the costs of a qualified rehab stay:

  • Days 1 through 20: Covered by Medicare at 100%
  • Days 21 through 100: Daily copay required (if you still qualify)
  • After day 100: Full responsibility for costs

Contact your secondary insurance provider to determine if they will cover the copay.

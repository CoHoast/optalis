Understanding Assisted Living

What Is Assisted Living?

An assisted living community is not a nursing home — it offers a different lifestyle. An assisted living community is for seniors who need some assistance but are, for the most part, independent, while a nursing home or skilled nursing facility is more appropriate for seniors who have ongoing medical needs.

An assisted living community offers medical and living assistance, but they're also focused on providing a social environment with planned activities, physical wellness programs, and other convenient support services that help seniors continue to enjoy active, fulfilling lifestyles.

Key Difference:

Assisted Living = Independence with Support
Skilled Nursing = Medical Care Focus

Assisted Living Rehab and Recovery Plan

Select communities offer respite recovery suites, called Assisted Living Rehab & Recovery, to provide proactive care and rehabilitation for those transitioning from a hospital, same-day surgery center, or skilled center prior to returning home.

Program Includes:

  • Comfortable, fully-furnished apartment suite
  • 24/7 nursing support and personal care assistance
  • Medication management
  • On-site physical, speech, and occupational therapy
  • Full dining menus with room service options
  • Help with all activities of daily living

Perfect For:

  • Those denied skilled care through insurance
  • Exhausted health care benefits coverage
  • Still benefit from therapy and nursing services
  • Considering permanent assisted living

Memory Care at Cranberry Park

What Is Memory Care Like At Cranberry Park?

Our Michigan Cranberry Park communities offer memory care in a safe and supportive environment for residents living with cognitive decline, including Alzheimer's or other types of dementia.

Memory care in our communities is designed to provide a personalized plan of care for each resident, recognizing their unique care needs, interests, dining preferences, special diets, and more. We facilitate a variety of structured programs throughout the day to help minimize anxiety and agitation.
